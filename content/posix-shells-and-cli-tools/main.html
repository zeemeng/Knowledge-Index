This subject section only treats command-line shells and matters related to these. This subject section treats any command-line shell available on any computing system, as well as shell scripting features and other programs which may be used with or in said command-line shells.

However, the main focus of the present section is on the "Unix shell" and more specifically on the "Bourne Again Shell" (Bash), which is default command-line shell of many GNU/Linux distributions. As such, in the context of this subject section, the term shell when used without qualifiers or without notes which states otherwise, shall refer to the Bash shell.

Note that command-line programs which relate to configuring, installing and modifying the OS kernel and core OS features and modules of a Unix-like (e.g. GNU/Linux) system are not presented in this section. They are be presented in the subject section GNU/Linux and Friends .
